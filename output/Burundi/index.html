Burundi

Burundi clues

  • Language:

    Kirundi, French; Latin alphabet with no special characters

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue stripe at the top; the country code 'B' for Burundi is often displayed.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are often in French and Kirundi, with a mix of standard international symbols and local variations.

  • Environment:

    The landscape features rolling hills, green valleys, and is predominantly rural; there are also lakes such as Lake Tanganyika.

  • Infrastructure:

    Buildings typically feature a mix of traditional African styles and colonial architecture; urban areas have simple structures and some modern developments.

  • Culture:

    Look for cultural markers such as traditional markets, local crafts, and community gatherings; the presence of the drum is significant.

  • Bonus:

    Street scenes may show bicycles and motorbikes as common transport; signs of agriculture may be prevalent, with terraced farming visible.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of a white diagonal cross on a red background, with green triangles at the top and bottom; it features three stars in the center.
