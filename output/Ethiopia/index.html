Ethiopia

Ethiopia clues

  • Language:

    Amharic is the official language, using the Ge'ez script which features unique characters and is an abugida with consonant-vowel combinations.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue band at the top. The format typically includes a combination of letters and numbers.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs often feature Amharic script, with some also including English. Signs may have distinctive colors like blue and green.

  • Environment:

    The landscape varies from mountainous regions to plateaus and valleys. Common features include the Great Rift Valley and diverse vegetation.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architecture often includes a mix of traditional Ethiopian structures with thatched roofs and modern buildings in urban areas. Churches often feature unique designs.

  • Culture:

    Ethiopian cultural markers include traditional coffee ceremonies, unique cuisine (like injera), and religious festivals. Look for traditional clothing.

  • Bonus:

    Look for unique features like tuk-tuks, distinct local markets, and rural areas with traditional huts. Also, the presence of Ethiopian Orthodox churches.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of horizontal stripes of green, yellow, and red with a blue circle and a yellow star in the center.
