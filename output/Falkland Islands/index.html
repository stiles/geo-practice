Falkland Islands

Falkland Islands clues

  • Language:

    English is the official language. The alphabet is the same as in standard English with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue band on the left featuring the Union Jack and the words 'Falkland Islands'.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are in English, with clear, simple designs. Warning signs often have a yellow background.

  • Environment:

    The landscape is characterized by rugged coastlines, rolling hills, and a mix of grasslands and peat bogs. Wildlife such as penguins and seals is common.

  • Infrastructure:

    Buildings typically reflect British colonial architecture with low-rise structures, including wooden houses and some stone buildings.

  • Culture:

    British cultural influences are evident, with local customs and traditions reflecting a mix of British and local heritage.

  • Bonus:

    Remote locations may feature sparse infrastructure, and you might see sheep grazing, which is common in the islands.

  • Flag:

    The flag features a blue field with the Union Jack in the canton and the Falkland Islands coat of arms centered on the fly.
