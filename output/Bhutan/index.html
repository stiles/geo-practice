Bhutan

Bhutan clues

  • Language:

    Dzongkha is the official language, written in the Tibetan script. The script includes unique characters and diacritics.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a green border. The format typically includes a combination of letters and numbers, along with the country's code 'BT'.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs often feature both English and Dzongkha. Signs are typically colorful and may include traditional motifs.

  • Environment:

    Bhutan is characterized by mountainous landscapes, lush green valleys, and a mix of subtropical and alpine climates. The Himalayas dominate the northern region.

  • Infrastructure:

    Buildings often showcase traditional Bhutanese architecture, with wooden balconies, intricate carvings, and colorful paintings. Urban areas may have a mix of modern and traditional structures.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive cultural markers include prayer flags, stupas, and dzongs (fortified monasteries). Traditional dress, such as the gho for men and kira for women, can also be seen.

  • Bonus:

    Look for the presence of unique landscapes, such as terraced fields and fortified structures. The absence of major commercial logos and the presence of religious symbols are also notable.

  • Flag:

    The flag of Bhutan features a yellow upper half and a orange lower half, with a white dragon across the middle holding jewels in its claws.
