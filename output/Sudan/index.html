Sudan

Sudan clues

  • Language:

    Arabic is the official language, using the Arabic script. English is also widely understood.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue border; the first number indicates the state or region.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are primarily in Arabic, with some signs also in English, often featuring Arabic script alongside Latin alphabet.

  • Environment:

    The landscape is characterized by deserts, particularly the Nubian Desert, and the Nile River running through the country.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles include a mix of traditional Sudanese mud brick buildings, modern concrete structures, and Islamic architecture.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive markers include traditional markets (souks), Sudanese coffee houses, and local customs such as traditional clothing like the tobe.

  • Bonus:

    Look for palm trees, traditional mud-brick architecture, and the presence of the Nile River in urban areas.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of three horizontal stripes of red, white, and black, with a green triangle on the left side.
