Cyprus

Cyprus clues

  • Language:

    Greek and Turkish; Greek uses the Greek alphabet, while Turkish uses the Latin alphabet.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue strip on the left featuring the EU flag and the country code 'CY'.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are in both Greek and Turkish, with a mix of Latin and Greek alphabets. Signs often feature a green background for directions.

  • Environment:

    Cyprus has a mix of coastal areas, hills, and mountainous regions, with Mediterranean vegetation and distinct rocky landscapes.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles include a mix of Greek, Ottoman, and British influences. Look for traditional stone houses, modern buildings, and historical landmarks.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive markers include Greek Orthodox churches, Turkish mosques, and various cultural festivals reflecting both Greek and Turkish heritage.

  • Bonus:

    Distinctive flora such as olive trees and cacti; presence of both Greek and Turkish flags in various areas.

  • Flag:

    A white field with a copper-orange silhouette of the island of Cyprus and two olive branches underneath.
