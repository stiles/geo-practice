French Polynesia

French Polynesia clues

  • Language:

    French is the official language, with Tahitian also widely spoken. The alphabet is Latin-based, and may include accented characters such as é, è, and ê.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates in French Polynesia typically have a blue background with white characters and include a 'PF' prefix to indicate Polynesia.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are primarily in French, using standard French road sign shapes and colors. Look for signs with both French and Tahitian names.

  • Environment:

    French Polynesia features stunning tropical landscapes, including lush green mountains, coral reefs, and sandy beaches. The flora includes palm trees and vibrant flowers.

  • Infrastructure:

    Common architectural styles include traditional Polynesian structures with thatched roofs, as well as modern buildings influenced by French styles. Urban areas may have brightly colored houses.

  • Culture:

    Cultural markers include traditional Polynesian carvings, tiki statues, and local markets. The presence of boats, particularly outrigger canoes, is also a notable aspect.

  • Bonus:

    Look for distinct tropical vegetation, ocean views, and the presence of overwater bungalows which are iconic to the region.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of three horizontal stripes: red at the top and bottom, with a white stripe in the middle featuring a stylized image of a canoe and a sun.
