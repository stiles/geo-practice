Bangladesh

Bangladesh clues

  • Language:

    Bengali (Bangla) is the official language, written in the Bengali script, which has unique characters and diacritics.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are typically white with a blue or yellow stripe, featuring Bengali script and numbers.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are often in Bengali, with some bilingual signs in English. They may feature colorful designs and are usually rectangular.

  • Environment:

    The landscape includes lush greenery, rice fields, rivers, and urban areas with dense populations. The Sundarbans mangrove forest is a notable geographic feature.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles reflect a mix of colonial, modern, and traditional Bengali influences, with brick and concrete buildings prevalent in urban areas.

  • Culture:

    Look for rickshaws, street markets, and signs of local cuisine. Festivals and cultural motifs may also be visible.

  • Bonus:

    Distinctive clothing such as saris and lungis, as well as presence of mosques and temples, can be identifying features.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of a red circle on a green field, symbolizing the blood of those who fought for independence and the lush landscape of Bangladesh.
