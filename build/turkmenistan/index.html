Turkmenistan

Turkmenistan clues

  • Language:

    Turkmen (Latin script), with some letters like 'ň', 'ä', 'ö', 'ü', and 'ş' that are specific to the Turkmen alphabet.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue band at the top featuring the country code 'TM' and a sequence of numbers. The plates often have a green stripe indicating the region.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are typically in Turkmen and may also include Cyrillic script. They are often rectangular with white backgrounds and black or blue text.

  • Environment:

    The landscape is characterized by vast deserts, particularly the Karakum Desert, as well as mountainous regions in the east. The Caspian Sea borders the west.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architecture includes Soviet-style buildings, modern constructions, and traditional Turkmen yurt-style structures. Ashgabat features many grandiose buildings with white marble facades.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive cultural markers include traditional carpets, the presence of large monuments (like the Turkmenbashi Palace), and significant representation of horses, particularly the Akhal-Teke breed.

  • Bonus:

    Look for unique roadside features like the 'Gates of Hell' gas crater in Darvaza, as well as signs in Turkmen and the prevalence of the color white in urban areas.

  • Flag:

    The flag of Turkmenistan is green with a vertical red stripe containing five white stars and a crescent moon, and a traditional carpet design on the left.
