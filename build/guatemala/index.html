Guatemala

Guatemala clues

  • Language:

    Spanish is the official language, and the alphabet is Latin with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates typically have a white background with blue and yellow borders, featuring the word 'Guatemala' and a unique alphanumeric code.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are primarily in Spanish, with a mix of regulatory, warning, and informational signs; many signs may use pictograms.

  • Environment:

    The landscape includes volcanic mountains, dense jungles, and agriculture; there are also urban areas with colorful houses.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architecture features colonial Spanish influences, with vibrant colors, tiled roofs, and cobblestone streets in urban areas.

  • Culture:

    Look for traditional clothing, particularly the colorful huipils worn by indigenous people, and markets selling local crafts.

  • Bonus:

    Street View may show traditional festivals, indigenous markets, and significant archaeological sites like Tikal.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of three vertical stripes; two blue outer stripes and a white center stripe, with the Guatemalan coat of arms in the center.
