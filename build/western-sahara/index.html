Western Sahara

Western Sahara clues

  • Language:

    Arabic (official), Spanish (historical influence), Latin alphabet used in some contexts

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are typically white with a blue strip at the top; they may include Arabic and Spanish characters.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are often in Arabic and Spanish; signs may have a mix of styles, including simple and modern designs.

  • Environment:

    Predominantly desert landscape with sandy dunes, rocky plateaus, and sparse vegetation; coastal areas along the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architecture reflects a mix of Berber, Arab, and Spanish influences; towns may have low-rise buildings with flat roofs, and some areas feature traditional adobe structures.

  • Culture:

    Presence of Sahrawi cultural elements such as traditional tents (haimas), camel herding, and local festivals; distinctive jewelry and textiles may be evident.

  • Bonus:

    Sparse population density with open landscapes; occasional military presence in certain areas; distinctive nomadic lifestyle elements.

  • Flag:

    A horizontal tricolor of black, white, and green with a red star and crescent on the left side.
