Côte d'Ivoire (Ivory Coast)

Côte d'Ivoire (Ivory Coast) clues

  • Language:

    French is the official language, using the Latin alphabet without special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are typically white with a blue band at the top featuring the Ivorian flag and the number 'Côte d'Ivoire'.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are often in French, featuring blue, green, and white colors. Stop signs are octagonal and red.

  • Environment:

    The landscape features a mix of coastal areas, savannahs, and rainforests, with notable palm trees along the coast.

  • Infrastructure:

    Urban areas feature modern buildings alongside traditional architecture, with a mix of concrete and colorful facades.

  • Culture:

    Look for vibrant markets, traditional clothing, and local art such as wood carvings and textiles.

  • Bonus:

    Presence of distinctive public transport vehicles like 'gbaka' (shared minibuses) and 'brouhaha' (motorbike taxis).

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of three vertical stripes: orange (hoist side), white (middle), and green.
