British Virgin Islands

British Virgin Islands clues

  • Language:

    English is the official language; the alphabet is the standard Latin alphabet with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates usually have a white background with black text, featuring a prefix that indicates the territory.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are generally in English, with a mix of standard British and local road signage styles.

  • Environment:

    The landscape is characterized by mountainous terrain, lush greenery, and coastal areas with beautiful beaches.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles include a mix of colonial and modern Caribbean designs, often featuring pastel colors and wooden balconies.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive markers include local festivals, food stalls with traditional Caribbean cuisine, and signs of British colonial influence.

  • Bonus:

    Look for the presence of palm trees, tropical plants, and the general vibe of a laid-back island lifestyle.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of a blue field with the Union Jack in the canton and the coat of arms of the British Virgin Islands on the fly side.
