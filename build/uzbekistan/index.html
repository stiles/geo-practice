Uzbekistan

Uzbekistan clues

  • Language:

    Uzbek is the official language, using the Latin alphabet with some additional characters such as 'ў', 'ғ', and 'қ'.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with blue and yellow accents, often featuring regional codes.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are typically in Uzbek, with some bilingual signs in Russian; signs are often rectangular with a blue or white background.

  • Environment:

    Uzbekistan features a mix of deserts, mountains, and steppes, with notable landscapes like the Kyzylkum Desert and the Tien Shan mountains.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles range from Soviet-era buildings to traditional Islamic architecture, including mosques with intricate tile work.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive markers include bazaars, traditional yurts, and cultural symbols like the 'Samarkand' pattern in tile art.

  • Bonus:

    Look for the presence of cotton fields, large public squares, and monuments commemorating historical figures.

  • Flag:

    The flag features three horizontal stripes of blue, white, and green, with a red stripe in the middle, and crescent moon and stars on the left.
