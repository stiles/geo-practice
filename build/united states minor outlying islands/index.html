United States Minor Outlying Islands clues

  • Language:

    English is predominantly spoken; the alphabet is the standard Latin alphabet with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are typically white with blue lettering, often featuring the name of the territory.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are generally in English and may have a mix of US-style and local variations; road signs are often simple and easy to read.

  • Environment:

    The landscape features tropical islands with sandy beaches, coral reefs, and a mix of palm trees and other tropical flora.

  • Infrastructure:

    Buildings are often low-rise, with a mix of modern and traditional Caribbean architecture; urban areas may include community centers and local shops.

  • Culture:

    Cultural markers include local festivals, traditional crafts, and influences from American and Pacific Islander cultures.

  • Bonus:

    Look for unique landmarks, local flora, and fauna, and the overall tropical ambiance; presence of American-style conveniences.

  • Flag:

    The flag is a blue field with a white star in the center, representing the United States, alongside a red stripe at the bottom.
