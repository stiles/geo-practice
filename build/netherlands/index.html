Netherlands

Netherlands clues

  • Language:

    Dutch; uses the Latin alphabet, with some special characters like å, é, and ë.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates have a combination of letters and numbers, typically in the format XX-99-99 or 99-99-99, with a blue EU strip on the left featuring the EU stars.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs often use white backgrounds with blue, green, or yellow text; directional signs are usually rectangular with clear symbols.

  • Environment:

    Flat landscapes with extensive farmland, canals, windmills, and occasional dikes; urban areas feature a mix of modern and historic buildings.

  • Infrastructure:

    Common architectural styles include traditional Dutch houses with gabled roofs, modernist buildings, and extensive cycling infrastructure such as dedicated bike lanes.

  • Culture:

    Look for bicycle culture, street markets, and signs in Dutch; also, distinctive items like tulips or windmills in the vicinity.

  • Bonus:

    Presence of many bicycles, canal systems, and the use of Dutch names for places and streets.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of three horizontal bands of red, white, and blue.
