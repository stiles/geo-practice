Solomon Islands

Solomon Islands clues

  • Language:

    The official language is English, but many local languages and dialects are spoken, including Pijin, which uses the Latin alphabet without special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates typically have a white background with black characters. The format usually includes a letter code followed by a series of numbers.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are often in English with a mix of local languages, featuring standard shapes like rectangles and circles. Warning signs may have pictograms.

  • Environment:

    The landscape features lush tropical forests, mountainous terrain, and coastal areas with beaches. There are many islands with varying topographies.

  • Infrastructure:

    Buildings often have a mix of traditional and colonial architecture, with many structures made from wood and thatch. Urban areas may have concrete buildings.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive markers include traditional markets, local crafts, and cultural events. Tribal art and carvings are common in many areas.

  • Bonus:

    Look for palm trees, coastal views, and remote island characteristics. The presence of traditional canoes or fishing boats can also be indicative.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of a blue field with a yellow diagonal cross, and a green triangle on the hoist side containing five white stars.
