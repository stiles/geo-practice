Cambodia

Cambodia clues

  • Language:

    Khmer, uses the Khmer script which is an abugida with distinct characters and no spaces between words.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue border for private vehicles, and they display a combination of letters and numbers.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are often in Khmer, with some bilingual signs in English. Look for unique shapes and colors, often rectangular or square.

  • Environment:

    Cambodia features lush landscapes, rice paddies, and rural areas, with the Tonle Sap lake and the Mekong River being significant geographical features.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architecture includes traditional Khmer styles like temples (e.g., Angkor Wat) and French colonial buildings in urban areas.

  • Culture:

    Look for markets, street food stalls, and Buddhist temples which are common cultural markers.

  • Bonus:

    Street View may show traditional wooden houses on stilts, palm trees, and rural scenes that reflect local life.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of three horizontal stripes: blue at the top and bottom, and red in the middle, with a depiction of Angkor Wat in white.
