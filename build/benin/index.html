Benin

Benin clues

  • Language:

    French is the official language. The alphabet is Latin-based, with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue stripe at the top and feature a format of two letters followed by four numbers.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are typically in French, with standard shapes and international symbols. They may feature a mix of colors, including blue for informational signs and red for prohibitory signs.

  • Environment:

    The landscape includes savannahs, tropical forests, and coastal regions along the Atlantic Ocean. The terrain is mostly flat with some hills in the north.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architecture features a mix of traditional mud huts, colonial-era buildings, and modern structures. Urban areas may have vibrant markets and colorful storefronts.

  • Culture:

    Cultural markers include the presence of voodoo temples, colorful textiles, and lively markets. Festivals and traditional music are also significant.

  • Bonus:

    Look for street vendors, bicycles, and motorcycles, which are common modes of transport. The presence of palm trees and a tropical climate can also be indicators.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of two horizontal stripes of yellow and red with a vertical green stripe on the hoist side.
