Liberia

Liberia clues

  • Language:

    English is the official language. The alphabet is the standard Latin alphabet with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue stripe at the top and often feature a design that includes the Liberian coat of arms.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are often in English, with a mix of standard international symbols. Signs may show wear and tear due to the climate.

  • Environment:

    The landscape features a mix of coastal areas, rainforests, and hilly terrain. There are often palm trees and tropical vegetation.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles include colonial buildings, traditional thatched-roof homes, and modern constructions in urban areas. Streets in cities may be paved, while rural areas might have unpaved roads.

  • Culture:

    Look for signs of local culture such as traditional markets, public gatherings, and street vendors. Cultural attire may also be observed.

  • Bonus:

    You may see the presence of motorcycles as common transport, as well as street vendors selling local food and products.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of 11 horizontal stripes alternating red and white, with a blue square in the canton containing a single white star.
