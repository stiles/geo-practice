Vatican City (Holy See)

Vatican City (Holy See) clues

  • Language:

    Italian is the official language. The alphabet is Latin with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue strip on the left side containing the letters 'I' for Italy.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs generally follow Italian standards; they are often bilingual with Italian and Latin in some areas.

  • Environment:

    Vatican City is an urban area with limited green spaces, characterized by historical architecture and cobblestone streets.

  • Infrastructure:

    The architecture is predominantly Renaissance and Baroque, with notable structures like St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive markers include religious symbols, papal insignia, and the presence of clergy in traditional attire.

  • Bonus:

    Look for the presence of the Swiss Guard and the iconic dome of St. Peter's Basilica.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of two vertical bands, yellow (hoist side) and white, with the crossed keys of Saint Peter and the Papal Tiara in the white band.
