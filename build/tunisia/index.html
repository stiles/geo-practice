Tunisia

Tunisia clues

  • Language:

    Arabic is the official language, and French is widely spoken. The Arabic alphabet is used, which includes special characters like the letters ع, غ, and ق.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue stripe at the top featuring the letters 'TN' for Tunisia.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are typically in Arabic and French. They may include directional signs, speed limits, and road hazard warnings, often using a mix of both languages.

  • Environment:

    Tunisia features a mix of coastal areas, desert landscapes (Sahara), and mountainous regions (Nefzaoua). The Mediterranean coastline is prominent.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles include a mix of traditional Tunisian, French colonial, and modern buildings. Whitewashed buildings with colorful doors are common in coastal towns.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive markers include markets (souks), traditional cafes, and the presence of olive trees. Cultural symbols like mosaics may be visible in some areas.

  • Bonus:

    Look for olive groves, traditional Tunisian doors, and the presence of historical sites like ruins of Carthage or Roman amphitheaters.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of a red field with a white circle in the center, containing a red crescent and a five-pointed star.
