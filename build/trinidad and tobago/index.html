Trinidad and Tobago

Trinidad and Tobago clues

  • Language:

    English is the official language, with Trinidadian Creole and Tobagonian Creole also spoken. The alphabet is Latin with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue border and feature a combination of letters and numbers. The format includes a two-letter prefix indicating the type of vehicle.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are typically in English, using standard shapes and colors found in the UK, including warning signs in yellow and regulatory signs in red.

  • Environment:

    The landscape includes coastal areas, lush rainforests, and mountainous regions, with a tropical climate and diverse flora and fauna.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles feature a mix of colonial buildings, modern structures, and traditional wooden houses, with vibrant colors common in urban areas.

  • Culture:

    Distinctive markers include carnival celebrations, calypso music, and steelpan instruments, along with local cuisine featuring dishes like doubles and roti.

  • Bonus:

    Look for unique features such as street art, vibrant murals, and the presence of palm trees, as well as the occasional sighting of local wildlife.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of a red field with a black diagonal stripe bordered by white, symbolizing the country's resources and cultural heritage.
