Guinea-Bissau

Guinea-Bissau clues

  • Language:

    The official language is Portuguese, and Crioulo is widely spoken. The alphabet is Latin with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates typically have a blue band with the letters 'GB' for Guinea-Bissau.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are generally in Portuguese, with standard shapes and colors similar to those in other Portuguese-speaking countries.

  • Environment:

    The landscape includes coastal areas, mangroves, and a mix of savanna and forest. The terrain is generally flat.

  • Infrastructure:

    Buildings often feature colonial architecture mixed with modern styles, with a prevalence of concrete structures in urban areas.

  • Culture:

    Look for markers of local culture such as traditional markets, colorful clothing, and street vendors. Festivals and music can also be distinctive.

  • Bonus:

    Street View may show signs of tropical vegetation, local animals, and possibly scenes from the vibrant local fishing industry.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of two horizontal stripes of yellow and green, with a vertical red stripe on the left featuring a black star.
