DR Congo

DR Congo clues

  • Language:

    French is the official language; Lingala, Kikongo, Kiyaka, and Tshiluba are also spoken. The alphabet is Latin, with no special characters.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are typically yellow with black lettering. Plates often start with a two-letter prefix indicating the province.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are often in French, with distinctive blue and white colors. Informal signage may display local languages.

  • Environment:

    The landscape features a mix of dense rainforests, savannas, and the Congo River basin. The terrain can be hilly in certain regions.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architecture ranges from colonial-era buildings in urban areas to mud-brick houses in rural areas. Roads are often unpaved outside major cities.

  • Culture:

    Look for colorful markets, traditional clothing, and street vendors. Music and art are prominent in local culture.

  • Bonus:

    Street scenes may feature lush greenery, informal settlements, and a lack of modern urban infrastructure. Presence of motorcycles and bicycles as common transport.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of a sky blue field with a yellow star in the upper hoist corner and a diagonal red stripe bordered in yellow running from the lower hoist to the upper fly.
