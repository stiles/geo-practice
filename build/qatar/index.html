Qatar

Qatar clues

  • Language:

    Arabic is the official language, using the Arabic alphabet. English is also widely spoken.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are typically white with a red border. The registration number is displayed in Arabic and English.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right-hand side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are primarily in Arabic and English, with a mix of both languages present. Signs are often large and well-lit.

  • Environment:

    Qatar is characterized by its desert landscape, with sandy terrain, sparse vegetation, and coastal areas along the Persian Gulf.

  • Infrastructure:

    Modern architectural styles are prevalent, including skyscrapers and innovative buildings. Traditional Qatari architecture can be seen in older areas.

  • Culture:

    Look for cultural markers such as traditional markets (souqs), Islamic architecture, and public art that reflects Qatari heritage.

  • Bonus:

    The presence of palm trees, large shopping malls, and modern highways are distinctive features. Look for signs of rapid urban development.

  • Flag:

    The flag consists of a maroon field with a white serrated band on the hoist side.
