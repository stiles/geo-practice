Laos clues

  • Language:

    Lao (ລາວ), uses a unique script derived from the Tai script with special characters such as ກ, ຂ, ຄ, and ງ.

  • Cars:

    Vehicle registration plates are typically white with a green text and may include a province code, often in Lao script.

  • Driving:

    Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

  • Signs:

    Road signs are usually in Lao script, with some bilingual signs in English; they often feature simple designs and symbols.

  • Environment:

    The landscape is characterized by mountainous regions, lush greenery, and the Mekong River; rural areas may have rice paddies.

  • Infrastructure:

    Architectural styles include traditional Lao wooden houses, colonial-era buildings in cities like Vientiane, and Buddhist temples with tiered roofs.

  • Culture:

    Look for Buddhist temples, traditional markets, and local festivals; the presence of woven textiles and handicrafts is also common.

  • Bonus:

    Street View often shows rural scenes with water buffalo and village life, as well as mountainous backdrops.

  • Flag:

    The flag has three horizontal stripes: red, blue, and red, with a white circle in the center.
